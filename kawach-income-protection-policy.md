## **SECTION 1 \-  DEFINITIONS**

The following terms shall carry the meanings defined below throughout this document:

**1.1 "Kawach" / "Platform"** \-  The Kawach InsurTech platform, operated by NoName.exe, which facilitates parametric income protection policies for gig economy delivery workers.

**1.2 "Insurer"** \-  The licensed insurance company underwriting this product in partnership with Kawach. Name to be updated upon regulatory finalization. All insurance obligations are borne by the Insurer, not the Platform.

**1.3 "Policyholder" / "Rider"** \-  A registered Q-  commerce delivery worker (Blinkit / Zepto / affiliated platforms) who has purchased a valid Weekly Policy through the Kawach mobile application.

**1.4 "Policy"** \-  A single Weekly Income Protection Policy purchased by a Rider, covering a fixed 7-  day period as defined in Section 5\.

**1.5 "Premium"** \-  The weekly amount paid by the Rider to activate coverage, as determined by the Rider's Primary Zone Risk Tier and applicable Reputation Score adjustments.

**1.6 "Primary Delivery Zone"** \-  The 2km × 2km geographic grid cell in Bengaluru assigned to the Rider at registration, determined by the GPS centroid of their active work sessions. This is the sole zone against which disruption events are evaluated.

**1.7 "Disruption Event"** \-  A qualifying external environmental event that meets or exceeds the defined parametric trigger thresholds in the Rider's Primary Delivery Zone, as listed in Section 4\.

**1.8 "Active Work Session"** \-  A GPS-  verified period during which the Rider is logged into the Kawach app with location services enabled, physically present within or moving through their Primary Delivery Zone, and demonstrably engaged in delivery activity.

**1.9 "Parametric Trigger"** \-  An automated, threshold-  based mechanism by which a Disruption Event is confirmed using third-  party environmental API data, without requiring manual claim filing by the Rider.

**1.10 "Payout"** \-  The income compensation amount disbursed to the Rider following a confirmed Disruption Event, calculated using the Kawach Hybrid Payout Model and subject to applicable caps.

**1.11 "Waiting Period"** \-  The mandatory period of 12 to 24 hours following successful Premium payment, during which coverage is not yet active and no Payout will be made.

**1.12 "Reputation Score"** \-  A dynamic score (0–100) assigned to each Rider based on verified work behaviour, claim accuracy, fraud signal history, and policy continuity. Used to apply premium discounts or loadings.

**1.13 "Basis Risk"** \-  The inherent possibility that a Disruption Event is confirmed by environmental data but the Rider's actual income loss is lower than the calculated Payout, or vice versa. Kawach's Hybrid Payout Model is designed to minimise but not eliminate Basis Risk.

**1.14 "Loss of Income"** \-  The difference between the Rider's Expected Hourly Income (as computed by the Kawach Income Baseline Estimator for their zone and time of day) and the Rider's actual verified earnings during a confirmed Disruption Event period.

**1.15 "Lapse"** \-  The expiry of a Policy without renewal, resulting in a gap in coverage.

**1.16 "Fraud Flag"** \-  A system-  generated indicator applied to a Rider's account when anomalous behaviour is detected across GPS, sensor, or claim data, as detailed in Section 9\.

## **SECTION 3 \-  COVERAGE SCOPE**

**3.1 What This Policy Covers**

This Policy covers **verified loss of income** suffered by the Rider during a confirmed Disruption Event within their Primary Delivery Zone, during an Active Work Session, subject to all conditions in this document.

Coverage is strictly limited to income loss caused by the following and nothing else:

* An inability to complete deliveries due to impassable road conditions caused by urban flooding  
* A reduction in delivery activity directly attributable to confirmed heavy rainfall, extreme heat, severe air pollution, or severe thunderstorm conditions as defined in Section 4  
* Confirmed dispatch suppression on the delivery platform attributable to the Disruption Event (where verifiable via activity data)

**3.2 Nature of the Product**

This is a **parametric** product. Payouts are triggered by objectively measured environmental data crossing defined thresholds \-  not by the Rider submitting a manual claim or proving individual loss. The Rider accepts that:

* A Disruption Event may be confirmed and a Payout made even if their personal income loss was lower than the Payout amount  
* A Disruption Event may not be confirmed by the system even if the Rider subjectively felt disrupted, if environmental thresholds were not met  
* Kawach does not guarantee that every instance of income disruption will result in a Payout

**3.3 Geographic Limitation**

Coverage applies only to Disruption Events occurring in the Rider's registered Primary Delivery Zone. Events in adjacent zones, even if experienced by the Rider while transiting, are not covered unless the Rider's Primary Delivery Zone is also confirmed as disrupted.

**3.4 Temporal Limitation**

Coverage applies only during the 7-  day active coverage window of a valid Policy, after the Waiting Period has elapsed, and only during an Active Work Session as defined in Section 1.8.

## **SECTION 4 \-  QUALIFYING DISRUPTION EVENTS & TRIGGER THRESHOLDS**

A Disruption Event is confirmed only when **all three** of the following conditions are simultaneously met:

1. The environmental threshold for a qualifying event type is breached in the Rider's Primary Delivery Zone  
2. The Kawach zone disruption score (Environmental Score ≥ 0.6 AND Activity Drop ≥ 0.40) is confirmed  
3. The Rider is verified as present and active in the disruption zone for at least 50% of the event duration

**4.1 Qualifying Event Types and Thresholds**

| Event Type | Trigger Threshold | Monitoring Frequency | Typical Season (Bengaluru) |
| ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- |
| Heavy Rainfall | ≥ 50mm within any 3-  hour window | Every 15 minutes | June – September |
| Urban Flooding | ≥ 80mm rainfall \+ flood-  prone zone flag active | Every 15 minutes | June – September |
| Extreme Heat | ≥ 43°C sustained for ≥ 2 consecutive hours | Every 30 minutes | April – May |
| Severe Air Pollution | AQI ≥ 300 sustained for ≥ 6 consecutive hours | Every 1 hour | December – February |
| Severe Thunderstorm | Wind speed ≥ 60 km/h \+ active storm alert | Event-  based | Pre-  monsoon (March–May) |

**4.2 Data Sources**

All environmental trigger data is sourced exclusively from the following third-  party APIs: OpenWeatherMap (rainfall, temperature, wind), AQICN/CPCB (air quality index), and verified flood alert feeds. Kawach does not rely on rider-  reported conditions to confirm any trigger.

**4.3 Event Duration**

A Disruption Event begins at the timestamp when thresholds are first breached and ends at the timestamp when conditions fall below threshold levels for a sustained period of 30 minutes. Events separated by less than 30 minutes of below-  threshold conditions are treated as a single continuous event.

**4.4 One Event, One Payout**

A single continuous Disruption Event generates a maximum of one Payout per Rider, regardless of event duration. A new event may only be declared after conditions have returned to normal for at least 30 minutes.

## **SECTION 5 \-  POLICY PERIOD, WAITING PERIOD & ACTIVATION**

**5.1 Policy Duration**

Each Policy covers a fixed period of **7 calendar days** from the moment coverage becomes active (i.e., after the Waiting Period has elapsed). Policies do not auto-  renew. The Rider must actively purchase a new Policy for the following week.

**5.2 Waiting Period**

Upon successful Premium payment, a mandatory Waiting Period of **12 to 24 hours** applies before coverage becomes active. The exact duration is determined by the system based on current zone risk conditions at time of purchase:

* Standard conditions: 12-  hour Waiting Period  
* Elevated risk conditions (active weather watch or flood advisory in zone): 24-  hour Waiting Period

The purpose of the Waiting Period is to prevent adverse selection \-  the purchase of a Policy immediately before or during a known Disruption Event. No Payout will be made for any Disruption Event that begins during the Waiting Period, even if the event continues after coverage becomes active.

**5.3 Coverage Timeline Example**

* Rider pays Premium: Monday 10:00 AM  
* Waiting Period (12 hrs): Monday 10:00 AM → Tuesday 10:00 AM  
* Coverage Active: Tuesday 10:00 AM  
* Coverage Expires: Tuesday 10:00 AM \+ 7 days \= the following Tuesday 10:00 AM

**5.4 Premium Non-  Refundability**

Once the Waiting Period has begun, the Premium is non-  refundable under any circumstances, including but not limited to: the Rider choosing not to work during the coverage period, no Disruption Events occurring during the coverage period, the Rider's device malfunctioning, or network connectivity issues.

**6.4 Premium Lock-  In**

Once a Policy is purchased, the Premium paid and coverage tier are locked for that 7-  day period. Mid-  week changes to zone risk scores or Reputation Scores do not affect an active Policy.

## **SECTION 7 \-  PAYOUT CALCULATION & CAPS**

**7.1 Hybrid Payout Model**

Payouts are calculated using the Kawach Hybrid Payout Model, which combines three independent signals to minimise Basis Risk:

Hybrid Score \= (0.5 × Income Deviation) \+ (0.3 × Activity Drop) \+ (0.2 × Environmental Score)

Final Payout \= Expected Hourly Income × Hybrid Score × Disrupted Hours

**7.2 Payout Caps**

The following hard caps apply regardless of calculated Payout amount:

| Cap Type | Limit |
| ----- | ----- |
| Per hour | ₹100 |
| Per Disruption Event | ₹1,500 |
| Per Policy week | ₹2,000 |
| Per calendar month | ₹6,000 |

Weekly and monthly caps are not carried forward. Unused coverage limits from one week do not roll over to the next Policy period.

**7.3 Payout Timeline**

* Fraud checks pass → Payout disbursed within 2 hours of Disruption Event confirmation  
* Fraud score High → Payout held up to 24 hours pending review  
* Fraud score Critical → Payout blocked pending investigation (see Section 9\)

**7.4 Payout Method**

Payouts are disbursed via Razorpay to the Rider's registered UPI ID or linked bank account. Kawach is not responsible for delays caused by the Rider's bank, UPI provider, or incorrect account details provided at registration.

## **SECTION 8 \-  DEDUCTIBLES & CO-  PAYMENT**

**8.1 No Traditional Deductible**

Given the micro-  insurance nature of this product and the low weekly Premium, Kawach does not apply a traditional fixed deductible (i.e., a fixed amount the Rider must absorb before Payout begins).

**8.2 Implicit Threshold Deductible**

However, an implicit threshold deductible exists by design through the parametric trigger system. A Disruption Event is only confirmed when:

* Environmental thresholds are fully breached (not partially \-   e.g., 48mm rainfall does not trigger the 50mm threshold)  
* Zone disruption score ≥ 0.6  
* Rider Activity Drop ≥ 40%

Events that fall below these thresholds \-  even if the Rider experienced minor disruption \-  do not generate a Payout. This functions as a deductible equivalent, ensuring only material income disruptions are compensated.

**8.3 Co-  Payment Structure**

A co-  payment applies on a sliding scale tied to the Rider's Reputation Score. This incentivises platform engagement and honest behaviour:

| Reputation Tier | Co-  Payment (Rider absorbs) | Kawach pays |
| :---: | :---: | :---: |
| Trusted (80–100) | 0% | 100% of calculated Payout |
| Established (60–79) | 5% | 95% of calculated Payout |
| Building (40–59) | 10% | 90% of calculated Payout |
| Developing (20–39) | 15% | 85% of calculated Payout |
| Restricted (0–19) | 20% | 80% of calculated Payout |

**Example:** A Rider in the "Building" tier receives a calculated Payout of ₹256. Their co-  payment is 10% (₹25.60), so they receive ₹230.40.

**8.4 Co-  Payment Rationale**

The co-  payment is not a financial hardship mechanism \-   it is a behavioural alignment tool. Riders who engage consistently with the platform, maintain honest GPS sessions, and renew policies regularly will quickly move to Trusted tier and face zero co-  payment.

## **SECTION 9 \-  FRAUD, MISREPRESENTATION & ANTI-  SPOOFING**

**9.1 Zero Tolerance Policy**

Kawach operates a zero-  tolerance policy toward fraud. Any attempt to manipulate, deceive, or exploit the claims system \-   whether individually or in coordination with others will result in immediate consequences as defined in this Section.

**9.2 Actions Constituting Fraud**

The following are explicitly defined as fraudulent acts under this Policy:

* Use of GPS spoofing applications or any software that reports false device location  
* Manipulation of device sensors (accelerometer, gyroscope) to simulate movement while stationary  
* Use of mock location providers or developer mode location overrides on Android/iOS devices  
* Coordinating with other Riders to simultaneously trigger false claims during a genuine Disruption Event  
* Providing false KYC information, platform credentials, or account details  
* Operating in a zone other than the registered Primary Delivery Zone while claiming zone-  based benefits  
* Submitting or triggering claims for Disruption Events during which the Rider was probably not working  
* Sharing account credentials with another person for the purpose of claim generation

**9.3 Fraud Detection Signals**

The system evaluates the following signals continuously during Active Work Sessions:

* Accelerometer and gyroscope variance (physical stillness while GPS reports movement)  
* Cell tower triangulation cross-  referenced against reported GPS coordinates  
* Battery drain rate and device thermal signature  
* GPS update interval regularity (spoofing apps produce unnaturally regular 1Hz intervals)  
* Mock location provider flag at device level  
* Dark store dwell time and pickup route pattern matching  
* Claim surge rate within a zone (syndicate detection)  
* Device sensor metadata fingerprinting across concurrent claims  
* Zone entry timestamp clustering (coordinated simultaneous zone entry detection)  
* Historical zone affinity score (claiming from a zone the Rider has no session history in)

**9.4 Fraud Risk Score & System Response**

| Fraud Score | Signals Present | System Action |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| Low (0–30) | 0–1 weak signals | Payout proceeds, session logged |
| Medium (31–60) | 2–3 explainable signals | Payout proceeds, audit trail created |
| High (61–85) | Multiple correlated signals | Payout held 24 hrs, self-  declaration requested |
| Critical (86–100) | Coordinated signals \+ device fingerprint match | Payout blocked, account flagged for review |

**9.5 Self-  Declaration Process**

When a claim scores High, the Rider receives an in-  app notification requesting a one-  tap confirmation of their activity status. This is a verification step, not an accusation. Failure to respond within 12 hours results in the claim being escalated to manual review.

**9.6 Consequences of Confirmed Fraud**

Upon confirmation of fraudulent activity:

* The current Policy is immediately void with no refund of Premium  
* All pending Payouts are permanently cancelled  
* Any Payouts received in the 90 days prior to fraud confirmation are subject to full recovery  
* The Rider's account is permanently suspended  
* The Rider is permanently ineligible to purchase future Kawach Policies  
* In cases of coordinated syndicate fraud, Kawach reserves the right to pursue civil and criminal remedies under applicable Indian law, including the Information Technology Act, 2000 and the Indian Penal Code

**9.7 Protection of Honest Riders**

No single fraud signal results in a blocked Payout. Only a Critical composite score triggers a block. Legitimate disruptions to GPS signals (network dropout, device restart, battery death) are handled through a 3-  missed-  ping grace window before a session is marked as signal-  interrupted rather than fraudulent.

## **SECTION 10 \-  EXCLUSIONS (WHAT IS NOT COVERED)**

The following are explicitly excluded from coverage under this Policy. No Payout will be made in connection with any of the following:

**10.1 External / Force Majeure Exclusions**

* Acts of war, invasion, foreign enemy action, or armed conflict, whether declared or undeclared  
* Civil war, rebellion, revolution, insurrection, or military takeover  
* Government-  declared national emergencies unrelated to environmental conditions  
* Pandemics, epidemics, or public health emergencies declared by WHO, Government of India, or Karnataka State Government (including lockdowns, curfews, or movement restrictions issued thereunder)  
* Nuclear, radiological, chemical, or biological events of any kind  
* Earthquakes, tsunamis, or other geological events (not covered as these are not the 5 qualifying disruption types)

**10.2 Platform & Business Decision Exclusions**

* Reduction in order dispatch by Blinkit, Zepto, or affiliated platforms due to business decisions, algorithm changes, promotional pauses, or operational downtime unrelated to environmental conditions  
* Platform-  side technical outages, app failures, or payment gateway issues  
* Changes to delivery fee structures, incentive programmes, or commission rates by platforms  
* Rider deactivation, suspension, or termination by the delivery platform for any reason

**10.3 Personal & Behavioural Exclusions**

* Income loss due to the Rider's personal choice to stop working, rest, or take a break during a Disruption Event  
* Illness, injury, fatigue, or any health-  related inability to work (this Policy does not cover health)  
* Vehicle breakdown, puncture, fuel shortage, or mechanical failure  
* Traffic violations, legal detention, or police action  
* Personal disputes, domestic obligations, or any other personal circumstance

**10.4 Geographic & Temporal Exclusions**

* Disruption Events occurring outside the Rider's registered Primary Delivery Zone  
* Income loss occurring during the Waiting Period  
* Income loss occurring after Policy expiry  
* Income loss on days when no Active Work Session is recorded

**10.5 Financial Exclusions**

* Income loss exceeding the applicable weekly or per-  event Payout caps  
* Loss of tips, bonuses, incentives, surge pay, or any income component beyond the base Expected Hourly Income baseline  
* Consequential losses, including loan defaults, missed rent, or any downstream financial impact  
* Lost income from secondary platforms or jobs not registered with Kawach

**10.6 Insurance-  Specific Exclusions**

* Pre-  existing disruption events \-  any Disruption Event that began before the Waiting Period ended is excluded even if it continues into the active coverage period  
* Events that meet environmental thresholds but do not produce a confirmed zone disruption score ≥ 0.6  
* Duplicate claims for the same Disruption Event (one payout per rider per event, enforced at database level)

## **SECTION 11 \-  RENEWAL & LAPSE CONDITIONS**

**11.1 No Auto-  Renewal**

Kawach Policies do **not** automatically renew. At the end of each 7-  day coverage period, the Policy expires and coverage ceases. The Rider must actively purchase a new Policy through the app to maintain continuous coverage.

**11.2 Renewal Window**

A Rider may purchase their next Policy at any time \-  including before the current Policy expires. If purchased before expiry, the new Policy's Waiting Period begins immediately upon payment, and coverage under the new Policy begins only after the Waiting Period elapses (which may overlap with the tail end of the current Policy  this does not create double coverage or double Payout eligibility).

**11.3 Lapse Definition**

A Lapse occurs when a Rider's coverage expires and they do not purchase a new Policy within 7 days of expiry. A gap of more than 7 days constitutes a Lapse.

**11.4 Consequences of Lapse**

* No Payout is available for any Disruption Event occurring during the Lapse period  
* Upon purchasing a new Policy after a Lapse, the full Waiting Period applies again from the time of purchase  
* A Lapse of more than 4 consecutive weeks results in a reduction of the Rider's Policy Continuity sub-  score within the Reputation Score, which may affect their Premium tier

**11.5 Incentive for Continuous Renewal**

Riders who maintain uninterrupted weekly Policy renewals for 8 or more consecutive weeks receive the following benefits:

* Policy Continuity sub-  score maintained at 100, contributing positively to Reputation Score  
* Eligibility for Trusted tier faster (if other score components are strong)  
* Priority Payout processing (payouts processed within 1 hour rather than 2 hours)

**11.6 Zone Change on Renewal**

A Rider may request a Primary Delivery Zone change at the time of renewal (not mid-  Policy). Zone changes take effect from the start of the new Policy's coverage period. A zone change does not reset the Reputation Score but may change the applicable Premium tier.

## **SECTION 12 \-  POLICY CANCELLATION**

**12.1 Cancellation by the Rider**

A Rider may request cancellation of their Policy at any time through the Kawach app. The following conditions apply:

* **Cancellation during Waiting Period:** Full Premium refund, minus a ₹5 processing fee  
* **Cancellation within 24 hours of coverage becoming active (cooling-  off period):** 50% Premium refund, provided no Disruption Event has been triggered during the coverage period  
* **Cancellation after 24 hours of active coverage:** No refund. The Policy remains active until its natural expiry date

**12.2 Cancellation by Kawach**

Kawach or the Insurer reserves the right to cancel a Policy under the following circumstances:

* Discovery of material misrepresentation in the Rider's KYC or registration information  
* Confirmed fraudulent activity (see Section 9.6)  
* Rider account status changes to Suspended or Permanently Banned  
* Discovery that the Rider does not meet eligibility criteria (Section 2\)  
* Regulatory direction from IRDAI or any competent authority

In cases of Kawach-  initiated cancellation due to fraud or misrepresentation, no Premium refund will be issued. In cases of cancellation due to regulatory direction or eligibility error not caused by the Rider, a pro-  rated Premium refund will be issued.

**12.3 Effect of Cancellation on Pending Payouts**

* If a Policy is cancelled due to fraud, all pending Payouts are immediately forfeited and previously paid Payouts may be recovered (Section 9.6)  
* If a Policy is cancelled for non-  fraud reasons during the cooling-  off period, any Payouts already disbursed during the active coverage period will be deducted from the refund amount

**12.4 Cancellation of Platform Partnership**

In the event that Kawach's partnership with the underwriting Insurer is terminated, all active Policies will be honoured until their natural expiry date. Riders will be notified at least 7 days in advance and offered a pro-  rated refund if they choose to cancel early.
